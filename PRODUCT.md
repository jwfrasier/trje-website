# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in the middle of a Christian faith journey: new believers finding footing, and longtime believers who have hit "the wall" (spiritual struggle, doubt, fatigue, transition). Many arrive through podcast platforms (YouTube, Spotify, Apple, Instagram) and come to the site to learn who TRJE is, listen, watch, or get in touch. State of mind is often searching, sometimes weary, sometimes hopeful. They are not shopping; they are looking for something real.

## Product Purpose

The Real Jesus Experience (TRJE) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit ministry and podcast that uplifts and supports believers through honest, thought-provoking conversations about faith, struggle, and transformation. The website is the ministry's home base: where the podcast lives, where the hosts and mission are introduced, where new visitors decide whether to listen further or connect. Success looks like a visitor finishing the homepage with a clear sense of what TRJE is, a desire to press play on an episode, and trust that they have arrived somewhere serious and welcoming.

## Brand Personality

Considered, exact, warm. The voice is unhurried and confident. Reads like a thoughtful magazine essay or a well-produced public-radio show, not a worship-night flyer. Speaks to the listener as a peer mid-journey, never as a target to convert. Three-word personality: *considered, warm, true*. Emotional goals: invitation, recognition (you are not alone at the wall), seriousness without austerity.

## Anti-references

What this should NOT look like:

- **Megachurch / evangelical web template.** Hero photo of a worship band on stage, gradient overlays, "Plan Your Visit" CTA, sermon-series tile grid. The default Christian-website pattern.
- **Stock-warmth Christian aesthetic.** Sunlit hands, blurred prayer, watercolor crosses, script fonts paired with sans-serif on cream. The Etsy-ministry look.
- **Generic SaaS marketing.** Hero metric, three identical feature cards, gradient blobs, Inter on white. The trained-data default.
- **Template / cookie-cutter portfolio.** Squarespace energy. Anything that signals "1000 other sites use this."
- **Crypto / Web3 / neon-on-black.** Dark gradients, neon accents, glassmorphism.
- **Pentagram-cold gallery-wall.** Too austere. Editorial seriousness, yes; clinical detachment, no. The site has people in it.

## Design Principles

1. **Treat faith content with editorial care.** This site is closer to a magazine or public-radio property than to a church website. Typography, pacing, and image selection should signal seriousness and craft.
2. **Warmth through restraint, not decoration.** Invitation comes from generous space, human imagery, and unhurried rhythm, not from gradients, ornaments, or affirming stock photography.
3. **The listener is mid-journey.** Many visitors arrive tired or searching. Do not perform certainty at them. The interface should feel safe to sit with, not insistent.
4. **Press play is the primary act.** Episodes are the product. The site's job is to make listening feel obvious, dignified, and frictionless across platforms.
5. **Real people, real testimony.** Show the hosts and guests as actual people with the studio around them. No stock imagery, no symbolic abstractions standing in for human presence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast minimum across all text. Full keyboard navigation. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: any motion is opt-out by default for users who request it. Audio content (the podcast) must include accessible alternatives where feasible (transcripts on episode pages when available). Consider screen-reader-friendly labeling on platform links and play controls. Site copy avoids insider jargon that would exclude new believers or seekers.
